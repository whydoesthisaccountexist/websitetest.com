Themes of Biology

Scientists estimate that 40 million species of organisms inhabit the Earth.

But 2 million out of 40 million have been identified and named, and only a few thousand have been studied in ANY detail.

But, alot of the world of biology remains to be explored and studied.

The good news is that there are guideposts to help chart a course through this world. These guideposts are like themes that unify biology.

in this chapter, you will read about unifying biology themes that allow biologists to study the large diversity of life.

The Study of Life

The first life form probaly arose the Earth more than 3.5 billion years ago. Much evidence suggests that this first organism or living thing was a SINGLE cell, too small to be seen without the aid of a microscope.

For millions of years, single-celled organisms floating in the seas was the only life on Earth. No fish swam the oceans, no birds flew through the skys, and no snakes moved across the ground.

Over time, organisms changed. New kinds of organisms arose the Earth from older kinds and came to inhabit almost every region of the Earth.

Today, brilliantly colored birds glide through the trees of tropical rain forests. Odd-looking fish prowl in the dark depths of the oceans, emitting a strange red light that helps them locate their prey.

Giant sequoia trees tower over hundreds of feet above the ferns growing on forest floors.

Nearly blind moles burrow their ways underground searching for tiny insects to consume.

These and millions types of organisms form the diverse population of Earth.

Biology, The Science of Life is the study of all living things.

Biology includes the study of microscopic structure of living cells. It also includes the study of microscopic interactions of millions of organisms.

It includes the study of the life history of individual organisms as well as the collective history of all organisms.

The science of biology is as varied as the organisms as the organisms that are its subjects.
